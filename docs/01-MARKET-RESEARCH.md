## 1. Niche Landscape (April 2026)

The "AI for traders / finance automation" niche sits at the intersection of three high-intent categories:

| Sub-niche | Search Demand | Competition | New-Domain Friendly |
|---|---|---|---|
| AI trading tools | High | Very High | ❌ Hard head terms |
| Trading automation workflows | Medium | Medium | ✅ Yes |
| Trading productivity & journaling | Medium | Low | ✅✅ Excellent |
| AI for finance research (non-trading) | High | Medium-High | ⚠️ Partial |
| No-code trading systems | Low-Medium | Low | ✅✅ Excellent |
| AI prompts for traders/investors | Medium | Low | ✅✅ Excellent |
| Market data + AI workflows | Medium | Medium | ✅ Yes |
| Import/export business intelligence | Low | Very Low | ✅✅✅ Untapped |
| Wealth productivity & systems | Medium | Medium | ✅ Yes |

### Top Competitors (head terms — avoid direct combat)
- **Investopedia, Benzinga, TradingView Blog** — domain rating 80+, do not compete on head keywords for 12 months
- **TraderLion, BabyPips, StockCharts** — strong on educational head terms
- **AI tool aggregators (Toolify, Futurepedia)** — own "best AI for X" generic searches

### Where the Gap Is
1. **Specific trader workflows** — "how to automate stock screener with chatgpt" has near-zero quality content
2. **AI prompt libraries for finance** — emerging, no dominant player
3. **No-code automation for retail traders** — Zapier/Make tutorials applied to trading
4. **Tool comparisons under $50/month** — affordable retail tier ignored by enterprise blogs
5. **Indian/SEA trader content in English** — India retail trading exploded post-2024, English content for this segment is thin
6. **"Import-export intelligence" + AI** — almost no English content, high commercial intent

## 6. Brand Tone & Voice

- **Pragmatic** — show, don't preach. Every article ends with something the reader can do today.
- **Plain-spoken** — no jargon walls. Define every acronym on first use.
- **Honest** — call out when a tool is overhyped or when a workflow doesn't justify the cost.
- **Confident, not cocky** — first-person ("I tested this for two weeks…") builds trust faster than corporate voice.
- **Numbers > adjectives** — "saved 4 hours/week" beats "incredibly powerful."

**Avoid:** Hype words ("revolutionary", "game-changing"), guru-speak, false urgency, generic listicles without testing.

## 7. 90-Day Growth Roadmap

### Days 1–14: Foundation
- ☐ Site live, all core pages, sitemap submitted to GSC + Bing
- ☐ 5 cornerstone "pillar" posts published (one per cluster)
- ☐ Newsletter live with magnet (e.g., "50 ChatGPT Prompts for Traders" PDF)
- ☐ Google Analytics 4 + Search Console connected
- ☐ Internal linking templates ready

### Days 15–45: Velocity
- ☐ Publish 3 posts/week (mix: 1 affiliate, 1 tutorial, 1 pillar/cluster supporter)
- ☐ Build out comparison content (highest commercial intent, fastest ranking)
- ☐ Begin affiliate applications (TradingView, Benzinga, Notion, etc.)
- ☐ Start collecting emails

### Days 46–75: Authority Signals
- ☐ Outreach for guest posts on 3 niche blogs (trader subreddits, finance forums)
- ☐ HARO / source-of-the-week submissions in finance niche
- ☐ Publish first comparison roundup post (e.g., "7 best AI trading journals tested")
- ☐ Launch lead magnet PDF

### Days 76–90: Optimization
- ☐ First content audit: which posts are gaining impressions in GSC? Refresh those.
- ☐ Add internal links from newer posts to early pillars
- ☐ Apply for first display ad network (Ezoic eligible at 10k pv/mo, but apply early)
- ☐ Launch first digital product (prompt pack, $9–19)

### Targets by Day 90:
- 35–40 indexed posts
- 1,000–3,000 monthly organic visitors
- 200+ email subscribers
- 5–10 affiliate programs approved
- 1 digital product live
